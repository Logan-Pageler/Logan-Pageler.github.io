ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE:

  I first started experimenting with Artificial Intelligence at the start of my sophomore year of highschool. My first project was a very simple evolutionary AI programmed from scratch in Java. I trained a group of dots to slowly travel the length of the screen, all while avoiding obstacles. My eyes lit up when they first made it to the goal. However, I quickly became bored with the dots and moved to more advance algorithms.

Image on its way. (Pardon my dust)

  I moved on to Neural Networks. My first iteration was supervised learning Neural Network that used gradient decent to increase it's accuracy. The entire project was also programmed from scratch in java. I trained the network to recognized checkerboard patterns and binary numbers. I about fell out of my seat the night it finally got a 95% accuracy on checkerboards. However, I quickly ran out of tests for it so I decided to give it an upgrade.

  My next goal was to perform unsupervised learning through Q-Learning and my existing neural network. It took me several month of perseverance to get the model to function properly. The only application I trained it to do was to drift around a track I had made. I couldn't believe my eyes when I woke up one morning (after letting my computer train all night for the millionth time) to seeing a car zipping around a track all on its own.

  Nowadays, I rely on libraries for my AI projects. I have built and worked with a variety of AIs to categorize Images, recognize text, understand speach, and much more. However, since starting to use libraries, these projects have become much easier, so I have decided to combine them all into one mega-assitant AI.

  My next project I have been working on is a smart home AI that can adjust to my behaviors. There's many features I want to implement, but for starters I want it to be able to recognize when someone introduces themselves, and create a profile for them to recognize them by face and voice. My next goal would be to add room following, where it knows what room of the house people are in. Then it can do things like make their music follow them. My two biggest hiccups right now are time, and also I wanted to switch from TensorFlow to PyTorch since it is much better supported.

  Shapemon was my teams final project for the class introduction to computer programming in my freshman year of high school. The code is definitely a mess, and this project was a huge learning experience but none the less, the game is pretty fun.

Although I have made a few changes since its submission (fixing game breaking bugs) but I tried to leave the original structure the same. (As much as hurts because of its messiness).

  While Shapemon may not be a good representation of my current programming capabilities, it is definitely an amazing representation of my passion for coding. With very limited tools under my belt, I spent multitudes of hours to finish the project in under just one month. I easily coded away most of my spring break that year. I spent on average 4 hours programming every day for that month, on top of my other school work. I easily stayed up past midnight most nights to work on it. Ultimately the project is one I'm quite proud of, (even though it pains me to know that I stuffed 12k lines into 1 file) and I hope to work on many more large scale projects in the future.
